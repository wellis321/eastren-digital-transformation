Introduction & Vision

1 minute
“I will deliver measurable improvements in repairs productivity and tenant experience within 20 months, through a phased, risk-managed programme governed by clear accountability and driven by resident co-design.”

Personal Framing

  • Digital transformation leader bringing structured, accountable delivery to complex public sector programmes
  • Track record of translating strategy into operational reality through phased, user-centred delivery
  • Experience bridging technical delivery teams, operational services, and senior stakeholders
  • Committed to genuine co-design with the people who use services, not just consultation
  • MSP programme management thinking within an agile delivery model

The Opportunity

  • Housing Repairs is a high-frequency, high-impact service — one of the most tangible touchpoints between council and tenant
  • Getting it right transforms tenant trust; getting it wrong is visible and damaging
  • ERC already has strong digital foundations to build on: M365, Goss CXM, SAtSD capability, 30+ Digital Champions, MAP programme governance
  • This programme sits at the intersection of tenant experience, workforce productivity, and legacy modernisation — exactly the right time to get it right
  • National policy tailwinds: Housing to 2040, Digital Scotland, mygov.scot platform
2

Understanding the Challenge

2 minutes

Current State Pain Points

  • Legacy ICT systems: Housing management system not integrated with repairs scheduling or mobile workforce tools; rekeying data between systems
  • No tenant self-service: Repairs must be reported by phone or in-person; no online portal or status tracking for tenants
  • Paper-heavy processes: Job cards, inspection reports, and compliance records reliant on manual processes
  • Limited mobile access: Field-based repairs staff cannot update job status in real time; updates batch-processed at end of day
  • Reactive maintenance model: Limited use of sensor data or demand analytics to predict and prevent failures
  • Fragmented data: Tenant, property, and repairs data held in separate systems with no “golden record” view
  • Scheduling inefficiency: Manual or semi-manual job allocation; no AI-assisted route/trade optimisation

ERC's Existing Digital Foundations

  • Microsoft 365: 2,000+ staff on M365 since 2020; Teams as primary collaboration tool; Power BI for data analytics
  • Goss CXM: Customer experience management platform in place; digital forms migration underway from legacy systems
  • MyEastRen portal: mygov.scot SSO citizen portal live; tenant account infrastructure already exists
  • Digital Champions Network: 30+ champions across departments; 100+ staff trained in SAtSD and user research with Scottish Digital Academy
  • IoT precedent: 50 housing properties already fitted with humidity/temperature sensors for damp/mould monitoring — proven technology foundation
  • MAP Programme: Modern, Ambitious Programme governance already established; Housing digital sits within existing programme structure
  • HAMP 2024–26: Digital Repairs project already scoped within Housing Asset Management Plan

Key Insight: Build on What Exists

ERC is not starting from zero. The strategic foundations, governance structures, technical platforms and digital skills are in place. The task is to harness these assets into a coherent programme with clear accountability, tenant involvement, and measurable outcomes — not to reinvent the wheel.

Vision & Success Outcomes

1.5 minutes

For Tenants

  • Report a repair online, 24/7, in under 3 minutes — from any device
  • Real-time status updates at every stage of the job lifecycle
  • Choose appointment slots; receive automated reminders
  • First-time fix rate improvement: right trade, right parts, right time
  • Satisfaction survey automatically sent post-repair; results visible to service managers
  • Phone and in-person channels retained for digitally excluded tenants
  • Accessible design: WCAG 2.1 AA minimum; translated into community languages where needed

For Staff

  • Mobile-enabled field workforce: job details, property history, compliance records on device
  • Automated scheduling optimisation: no more manual job allocation spreadsheets
  • No rekeying: data flows from tenant report through to job completion without duplication
  • Reduced admin burden: housing officers freed from routine chasing and manual reporting
  • Real-time dashboard: supervisors see live job status, SLA performance, and demand levels
  • Electronic job completion and compliance sign-off

Headline KPIs — Defined Upfront

30%+ Repairs reported digitally by Month 18
+10% First-time fix rate improvement vs baseline
1 day Admin time saved per housing officer per week
+15 pts Tenant satisfaction with repairs service

Governance & Programme Management

2 minutes

Programme Board Composition

  • Chair: Head of Housing (Senior Responsible Owner)
  • Housing ICT Manager
  • Digital Transformation Lead (programme delivery)
  • Finance Representative (benefits and budget oversight)
  • Change Manager (workforce change workstream)
  • Tenant Representative (tenant panel voice)
  • Procurement Representative (for Phase 1)
  • Communications Lead

Cadence

  • Daily stand-up: delivery team (sprint model)
  • Weekly: risk register review; delivery team sprint review
  • Fortnightly: steering group with programme highlights report
  • Monthly: Programme Board with full RAG dashboard
  • Quarterly: benefits tracking presentation to senior leadership

Programme Management Disciplines

  • Benefits owner: Named individual accountable for realising benefits — not the programme manager
  • Exception escalation: Clear protocol for what triggers escalation to Programme Board outside cycle
  • Change control: Formal change request process for scope changes; impact assessed on time, cost, benefits before approval
  • Risk register: Weekly review; owner, likelihood, impact, and mitigation for each risk
  • Issue log: Active issues tracked to resolution; no issue without an owner and target date
  • Dependency map: All inter-workstream dependencies logged and managed; procurement, ICT, change, comms aligned
  • Lessons learned: Not just at end — monthly retros embedded throughout
Key Lesson from Sector

Audit Scotland found that most council digital programme failures stem from unclear ownership, benefits not baselined before delivery, and change management treated as an afterthought. This programme addresses all three explicitly from day one.

Implementation Approach

1.5 minutes

Methodology

  • Agile delivery within programme governance: Two-week sprint cycles for build phases; MSP-style programme oversight at board level
  • SAtSD for all tenant-facing elements: Five stages (Discover → Define → Develop → Test → Implement) applied rigorously to the tenant portal and all resident-facing interactions
  • User research throughout: Real tenants, not assumptions — tenant panel recruited in Phase 0, involved at every design stage
  • Iterative prototyping: Clickable prototypes tested before build; no “big bang” delivery
  • Phased rollout: Alpha → Beta → Pilot → Full launch; each gate requires sign-off

Leveraging ERC's Assets

  • Goss CXM: Integrate repairs portal with existing CXM for unified service request view
  • mygov.scot SSO: Tenants log in with existing national account — no new password to remember; builds on MyEastRen investment
  • Microsoft 365: Power BI for programme KPI dashboards; Teams for delivery team collaboration; Power Automate for simple workflow automations
  • Digital Champions: Champions as peer-to-peer change agents for staff adoption; assigned to each workstream
  • Scottish Digital Academy: Continue building SAtSD and user research skills in housing staff during programme
  • IoT foundation: Existing 50-property sensor pilot as proof of concept for proactive maintenance expansion

Benefits Realisation

1.5 minutes

Benefits Framework

  • Baseline at Month 2: Current state metrics captured before any changes made — first-time fix rate, repairs reported by phone vs digital, admin time per officer, tenant satisfaction score
  • Leading indicators: Channel shift %, mobile app adoption rate, portal registration rate — tracked monthly from go-live
  • Lagging indicators: Tenant satisfaction, cost per repair, first-time fix rate — tracked quarterly, compared to baseline
  • Benefits owner: Named at outset; accountable to Programme Board; separate from programme delivery lead
  • Formal reviews: Month 12 interim benefits report; Month 20 final Benefits Realisation Report
  • Sector benchmarking: Results compared against Housemark sector data; published transparently

East Renfrewshire Council has established a network of more than 30 Digital Champions across the organisation to assist its staff in navigating new technologies
.
The ways these champions help the workforce include:
Technology Adoption: They play a key role in supporting employees to make the most of their technology, ensuring that staff members feel confident using the digital tools provided by the Council
.
Remote Working Support: During the pandemic, they were instrumental in helping the workforce transition to remote and hybrid working by providing guidance on the tools needed to work effectively from home
.
Specific Software Guidance: Digital Champions help colleagues use the Microsoft 365 suite for collaboration, specifically offering overviews and support for tools like Microsoft Teams and MS Planner
.
Training Assistance: They contribute to the Council's remote training efforts, which have seen sessions attended by over 100 staff members to improve their digital proficiency
.
Embedding New Cultures: By providing peer-to-peer support, they assist in shifting the organisational culture toward "building back better" and embracing more efficient, automated ways of working
.
Skill Development: Their efforts are a core part of the Council's workforce planning to build digital, data, and technology (DDaT) skills throughout the organisation
.
This network acts as a vital support system for the "Workforce Productivity" strand of the Council's digital strategy, which aims to increase the effectiveness of staff through the constant evolution of digital tools

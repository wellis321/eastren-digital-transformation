East Renfrewshire Council involves citizens in the design process primarily through the Scottish Approach to Service Design (SAtSD), which mandates that services be co-designed with the people who use them
.
The Council employs several specific methods to ensure community participation:
Direct User Engagement and Feedback
Customer Feedback Sessions: Throughout 2022/23, the Council held sessions to receive real-time feedback from residents during the design or redesign of processes
. This helps staff understand the specific problems customers face and what matters most to them
.
Remote Testing: During the pandemic, the Council used Microsoft Teams for remote testing of new systems, such as the Council Tax and Benefits project
. This allowed citizens to interact with platforms before they went live and identify what they liked, disliked, or wanted to be improved
.
Informing New Systems: Feedback from specific groups often leads to direct changes. For example, a new online registration system was built after 90% of new parents indicated they preferred digital booking over telephone calls
. Similarly, engagement regarding the Dams to Darnley country park resulted in changes to consent forms and the addition of real-time ticket availability on the booking page
.
Strategic and Representative Consultation
The Citizens Panel: The Council maintains a Citizens Panel to survey local opinions on service quality and attitudes toward specific issues, such as digital inclusion and the cost of living
.
Targeted Demographic Groups: To ensure diverse perspectives, the Council engages with Pupil Councils, parents' equality groups, the LGBT+ Youth Group, and various equalities networks when developing strategic plans like the "Vision for the Future"
.
Strategic Consultations: Public consultation is a routine part of major decision-making, including the development of the annual budget, the Children and Young People Plan, and the Climate Change Action Plan
.
Data-Driven Understanding
Locality Research: Beyond active sessions, the Council uses tailored data analytics and research (such as with Connecting Scotland beneficiaries) to build a deeper understanding of community vulnerabilities
. This allows the Council to design targeted support, such as Money Advice services, for specific postcodes identified as being at higher risk
.

# East Renfrewshire Council: Digital & Housing Transformation Strategic Notes

## 1. Strategic Vision and Governance
### Digital Transformation Strategy (2021-2024)
*   **Core Vision:** Moving forward on three key fronts: **customer experience**, **workforce productivity**, and **business systems and processes**.
*   **Modern, Ambitious Programme (MAP):** A major change framework seeking to develop council capabilities in modernising, digital, and data across six departmental programmes.
*   **Enabling Change:** The strategy is built on prioritising projects based on outcomes, focusing on benefits realisation (value vs. effort), and moving toward agile project methods for incremental delivery.

---

## 2. Customer Experience and Citizen Engagement
### Consistent Citizen Access
*   **MyEastRen Portal:** A single sign-on portal using **mygov.scot** to provide residents with a consistent "look and feel" across all services.
*   **Customer Experience Management (CXM):** Implementation of the **Goss platform** to migrate processes from legacy systems and align forms with Government Digital Service (GDS) standards.
*   **Advanced Interaction:** Exploration of AI, chatbots, live chat, and voice recognition to improve customer journeys based on preference.

### Co-Design with the Community
*   **Scottish Approach to Service Design (SAtSD):** Puts the user at the heart of design, using user research to ensure processes are inclusive and solve actual problems.
*   **Active Engagement:** The Council uses real-time feedback sessions and remote testing (via Microsoft Teams) to allow citizens to shape systems like Council Tax and Benefits before they go live.

---

## 3. Workforce Productivity and Skills
### Digital Champions Network
*   **Peer Support:** A network of over **30 Digital Champions** supports colleagues in adopting new technologies and navigating the transition to hybrid working.
*   **Training & Personas:** Over 100 staff have been trained in SAtSD and User Research. Training is targeted using "staff personas" to ensure skills are relevant to specific roles.

### Collaboration Tools
*   **Microsoft 365 (M365):** Full embedding of Tools like **Teams** and **MS Planner** to facilitate communication and remote working for over 2,000 staff.
*   **Automation:** Utilising Robotic Process Automation (RPA) and Lean principles to reduce manual rekeying and administrative duplication.

---

## 4. Frontline and Operational Technology
### Specialized Staff Tools
*   **Roads Service:** Use of **wearable technology** (watches) to monitor health and safety standards like hand-arm vibration, and handheld devices for mobile field maintenance.
*   **Waste Management:** Drivers of 25 bin lorries use **in-cab telematics** to automate job lists and completion notifications, saving over one day of admin time per week.
*   **Culture & Leisure:** Swimming teachers use tablets with the **Learn 2** system for real-time progress updates; libraries use "Open More" technology for out-of-hours access via swipe cards.
*   **Health and Social Care (HSCP):** Social workers and clinical teams use **Attend Anywhere** and **Teams** for virtual visiting and remote appointments.

### Internet of Things (IoT) Implementation
*   **Infrastructure:** Deployment of wireless sensors for **road temperature** (winter gritting) and monitoring **CO2/ventilation** in schools and council buildings.
*   **Housing Pilots:** Air quality sensors installed in 50 properties to monitor humidity and temperature, enabling **data-led early intervention** for damp and mould issues.

---

## 5. Data Strategy and Intelligence
### Proactive Service Delivery
*   **The "Golden Record":** A proof-of-concept project that matches data across multiple service lists (Housing, Shielding, Homecare) to create a single, accurate view of the customer.
*   **Vulnerability Mapping:** Using tailored data analytics to identify community vulnerabilities at a postcode level to target support like Money Advice.
*   **Performance Dashboards:** Utilising **Power BI** to provide real-time data to managers on service demands and staffing.

---

## 6. Housing Transformation
### Modernisation Projects
*   **Digital Repairs:** A dedicated project to enhance the productivity of Housing Repairs and improve tenant experience through digital modernization.
*   **System Replacement:** Transitioning from legacy housing ICT to a modern platform enabling mobile access for staff and a customer self-service portal.
*   **Digital Telecare:** Moving 3,000 vulnerable residents from analogue to **Digital Telecare** systems before the 2025 national switch-off.

### Housing Asset Management (HAMP 2024-2026)
*   **Stock Profile:** The Council manages 3,141 existing units.
*   **"Get to Zero":** Aiming for **EPC Band B** for all stock by 2032 through pilots in external wall insulation (EWI) and solar/battery storage.
*   **Strategic Challenges:** High construction cost inflation and the need to balance maintaining existing stock standards against the ambition to build 171 new units by 2028.

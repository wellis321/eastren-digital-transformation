Drawing on the sources, the following details expand on the technological tools used by frontline staff and the Council's broader strategic innovations in data, social inclusion, and workforce culture.
Frontline Staff Tools and Technology
The Council has deployed specific mobile and digital tools to support staff working directly in the community and in specialized facilities:
Environmental and Roads Services: Frontline workers in the Roads Service use wearable technology, such as watches that vibrate or sound an alarm if health and safety standards (like hand-arm vibration) are breached or if social distancing is required
. Additionally, officers use hand-held devices for mobile system access while performing lighting repairs and routine maintenance in the field
.
Waste Management: Drivers of the Council’s 25 bin lorries use in-cab telematics. This system sends a digital list of bins to be emptied directly to the operatives and automatically notifies supervisors when a job is completed or outstanding, which aids in route optimisation
.
Health and Social Care (HSCP): Social workers and care teams use mobile tools like WhatsApp, Zoom, and tablet devices to maintain contact with families and facilitate "virtual visiting" for care home residents
. Clinical staff use specialized platforms like "Attend Anywhere" and "Near Me" to conduct appointments and ward meetings remotely
.
Culture and Leisure: Swimming teachers use tablets or smartphones to mark student progress in real-time via the "Learn 2" system, which syncs at the end of lessons to provide parents with instant updates
. Library staff manage the "Open More" program, which uses technology to control lights and alarms and authenticates citizens via swipe cards for out-of-hours access
.
Advanced Technological Frontiers
Beyond standard tools, the Council is exploring advanced technologies to automate and improve service delivery:
AI and Automation: The Council is investigating the use of chatbots, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and voice recognition to create a multi-channel contact centre that allows residents to interact with services based on their preference
.
Internet of Things (IoT): A wireless sensor network is being used to harvest data for decision-making
. This includes road temperature sensors for winter gritting, CO2 and ventilation monitors in schools to ensure air quality, and smart street lighting
.
The "Golden Record": By matching data across different services (like shielding lists and property references), the Council is moving toward a "golden record" proof of concept. This aims to provide a single, accurate view of a citizen to proactively deliver services and reduce fraud
.
Data-Driven Social Support and Inclusion
The Council uses sophisticated data analysis to address inequalities and digital exclusion:
Financial Vulnerability Mapping: Through collaboration with the Smart Data Foundry, the Council overlays banking data with its own datasets to identify pockets of vulnerability at a postcode level
. This allows them to target Money Advice and Rights Team (MART) support to historically affluent areas where residents may be struggling with the cost of living
.
Digital Inclusion: To combat "digital exclusion," the Council has distributed over 2,000 devices (Chromebooks, iPads, and MiFi boxes) to low-income households and vulnerable groups
. All beneficiaries receive support from a Digital Champion to ensure they can effectively use the technology
.
Strategic Workforce Culture
The Council is shifting its internal culture to be more resilient and responsive:
Trauma-Informed Practice: The HSCP has established a Trauma Steering Group to transform the partnership into a trauma-responsive organization
. This involves using five key drivers to ensure policy and practice are aligned with principles of safety, trust, and empowerment
.
Hybrid Working: The organization is embedding hybrid working models (with staff typically working from home 40–60% of the time) to increase productivity and job satisfaction
.
Continuous Improvement: The "Business Systems & Processes" theme focuses on using Lean principles and Agile project methods to "chunk" projects, ensuring that benefits are realized quickly rather than waiting for long-term project completions
.

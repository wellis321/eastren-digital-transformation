Glossary

Key terms for housing digital transformation

A quick reference for acronyms and jargon I might use in the interview — to keep my answers clear, precise, and grounded in the right concepts.

Core programme & council terms

MAP — Modern, Ambitious Programme
ERC's umbrella change programme that brings six departmental transformation programmes under one governance model. The Housing Digital Transformation programme is one of those six — MAP is the “home” for this role.
HAMP — Housing Asset Management Plan
ERC's strategic plan for its 3,141 social housing units (currently HAMP 2024–26). Sets investment priorities, new build targets and identifies digital repairs as a priority project.
BAU — Business as Usual
The steady-state operating model after the programme ends. A good handover ensures processes, ownership, documentation and skills are in place so benefits are sustained.
KPI — Key Performance Indicator
Quantitative measures used to track programme and service performance (e.g. channel shift %, first-time fix rate, tenant satisfaction scores, admin hours saved).
RAG — Red / Amber / Green status
A traffic-light status used in programme reporting to show whether scope, time, cost and benefits are on track (green), at risk (amber) or off track (red). I use RAG dashboards to give a clear, honest picture of programme health.
SLA — Service Level Agreement
Agreed service targets (for example, response and completion times for emergency, urgent and routine repairs). Live dashboards track SLA performance and impending breaches.

Design, delivery & change

SAtSD — Scottish Approach to Service Design
Scotland's standard for designing public services. Follows five stages — Discover, Define, Develop, Test, Implement — and requires genuine co-design with service users.
GDS — Government Digital Service (UK)
The UK Government digital team and its service standard. While ERC follows SAtSD, GDS guidance is a useful comparative reference for delivery disciplines and technical standards.
PROSCI ADKAR
A widely used change management model: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement. Helps structure how staff are supported through change.
Agile delivery
Incremental delivery using short sprints, regular user feedback and iterative improvement, as opposed to a single “big bang” release. In this programme it sits within MAP governance.
Alpha / Beta / Live
Common digital delivery stages. Alpha explores options and prototypes; Beta pilots a near-complete service with real users; Live is the fully rolled-out service.
Discovery
The initial research phase where we understand user needs, current processes, systems and data. For this programme, Discovery happens in Phase 1 (Mobilisation & Discovery).

Technology, data & automation

OCR — Optical Character Recognition
Technology that converts scanned documents or images of text into machine-readable text. Could be used to digitise historic repairs records or paper forms.
RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation
An AI pattern where a model retrieves relevant documents or data at query time and then uses that material to generate an answer. In a housing context this could power staff or tenant chatbots that always respond from up-to-date, authoritative ERC policies and procedures.
IoT — Internet of Things
Networked sensors and devices (for example damp and mould sensors in properties) that can send data back to ERC to support proactive maintenance and safeguarding.
RPA — Robotic Process Automation
Software “bots” that automate repetitive, rules-based tasks (for example, moving data between systems or updating status codes) without changing core systems.
SSO — Single Sign-On
A mechanism that lets users log in once and access multiple services. For tenants, the mygov.scot SSO via MyEastRen avoids another username and password to remember.
CXM — Customer Experience Management platform
In ERC's case, Goss CXM — the platform used to manage digital forms, service requests and case management across services.
OCR, RAG & dashboards in context
These tools support the programme by reducing manual processing (OCR, RPA), making performance visible (RAG dashboards) and enabling earlier intervention (IoT + analytics).

Modern tools & platforms

Tools and platforms I use or reference when talking about modern delivery, automation and AI.

Claude
AI assistant (Anthropic) used for reasoning, writing and code. I use it to structure ideas, draft content and explore options — same way it can support staff with policy summaries or tenant-facing copy.
Cursor / GitHub Copilot
AI-powered coding tools that suggest code and edits inside the editor. Speeds up development and helps less technical staff script automations (e.g. Power Automate, spreadsheets) with guardrails.
DevOps
Culture and practices that combine development and operations: continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD), infrastructure as code, monitoring. Keeps digital services reliable and iterating quickly.
N8N / Zapier / Make
Low-code workflow and integration tools that connect apps and APIs without writing code. Useful for council teams to automate approvals, notifications and data flows between systems (e.g. repairs → housing system).
Llama (Meta) & open-source LLMs
Open-weight large language models that organisations can run on their own infrastructure. Relevant when discussing in-house AI, data sovereignty and cost control for future chatbots or assistive tools.
Notion / Confluence / SharePoint
Collaboration and knowledge-base tools for docs, wikis and project spaces. ERC already has M365 (SharePoint, Teams); tools like Notion are common in digital teams for runbooks, decision logs and handover.
Image generators (DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion)
AI that creates images from text prompts. Useful for concept visuals, training materials and inclusive imagery (e.g. tenant comms) without large photo budgets; need clear governance on use and rights.
Video generators (Sora, Runway, etc.)
AI that generates or edits video from text or clips. Emerging option for training, demos and tenant communications; I track capability and ethics (deepfakes, consent) when discussing digital channels.
OpenRouter / model APIs
APIs that let applications call multiple AI models (Claude, GPT, Llama, etc.) through one interface. Useful for prototyping and choosing the right model for each use case (e.g. summarisation vs. coding).
TensorFlow / PyTorch
Popular open-source machine learning frameworks. More relevant for data science and model training teams — I reference them to show awareness of the stack behind higher-level AI tools.
Browser-based AI
AI models that run directly in the browser (often on-device) without sending data to a server. An emerging option for privacy-preserving features like local summarisation or assistive tools on council devices.
Figma / Miro
Design and collaboration tools for interfaces (Figma) and workshops/diagrams (Miro). Standard in SAtSD and agile teams for prototyping and co-design with tenants and staff.

Housing & policy

EPC — Energy Performance Certificate
Standard rating of a property's energy efficiency. For ERC, the HAMP commits to achieving EPC Band B across the stock by 2032.
Housing to 2040
The Scottish Government's long-term housing strategy. Sets the policy backdrop for investment in housing quality, energy efficiency and tenant experience.
Digital telecare
The move from analogue to digital alarms and monitoring for vulnerable residents. ERC's digital housing architecture needs to align with this national programme.
Housemark
A sector benchmarking body providing performance comparisons for social landlords across the UK. Useful for setting and comparing KPI targets.

Understanding the Challenge

⏱ 2 minutes

⚠ Current State Pain Points

  • Legacy systems: Housing management not integrated with repairs scheduling or mobile tools; rekeying data between systems
  • No tenant self-service: Repairs must be reported by phone or in person; no online portal or status tracking
  • Paper-heavy processes: Job cards, inspection reports, compliance records all manual
  • Limited mobile access: Field staff cannot update job status in real time; updates batch-processed end of day
  • Reactive maintenance: Limited use of sensor data or demand analytics to predict failures
  • Fragmented data: Tenant, property, and repairs data held in separate systems; no “golden record” view
  • Scheduling inefficiency: Manual job allocation; no AI-assisted route or trade optimisation

✓ ERC’s Existing Digital Foundations

  • Microsoft 365: 2,000+ staff since 2020; Teams, Power BI already embedded
  • Goss CXM: Customer experience platform in place; digital forms migration underway
  • MyEastRen portal: mygov.scot SSO citizen portal live; tenant account infrastructure exists
  • Digital Champions: 30+ champions across departments; 100+ staff trained in SAtSD with Scottish Digital Academy
  • IoT precedent: 50 housing properties fitted with humidity/temperature sensors — proven technology foundation
  • MAP Programme: Modern, Ambitious Programme governance already established
  • HAMP 2024–26: Digital Repairs project already scoped within the Housing Asset Management Plan
Key Insight — Build on What Exists: ERC is not starting from zero. The strategic foundations, governance structures, technical platforms and digital skills are in place. The task is to harness these into a coherent programme with clear accountability, tenant involvement, and measurable outcomes.

Cadence

  • Daily: delivery team stand-up (sprint model)
  • Weekly: risk register review; sprint review
  • Fortnightly: steering group — programme highlights report
  • Monthly: Programme Board with full RAG dashboard
  • Quarterly: benefits tracking presentation to senior leadership

Management Disciplines

  • Benefits owner: Named individual accountable for benefits realisation — separate from programme manager
  • Exception protocol: Clear trigger for escalation outside normal cycle
  • Change control: Formal request process; scope changes assessed on time, cost, benefits before approval
  • Risk register: Weekly review; owner, likelihood, impact, and mitigation for each risk
  • Monthly retros: Lessons learned embedded throughout — not just at close
Sector lesson (Audit Scotland): Most council digital programme failures stem from unclear ownership, benefits not baselined before delivery, and change management treated as an afterthought. This programme addresses all three from day one.

Implementation Approach

⏱ 1.5 minutes

Methodology

  • Agile within programme governance: Two-week sprint cycles for build phases; MSP-style oversight at board level
  • SAtSD for all tenant-facing elements: Discover → Define → Develop → Test → Implement applied rigorously to the tenant portal
  • User research throughout: Real tenants recruited in Phase 1 — not assumed requirements
  • Iterative prototyping: Clickable prototypes tested before build; no “big bang” delivery
  • Phased gates: Alpha → Beta → Pilot → Full launch; each gate requires sign-off before proceeding

Leveraging ERC’s Existing Assets

  • Goss CXM: Integrate repairs portal with existing CXM for a unified service request view
  • mygov.scot SSO: Tenants log in with existing national account — no new password; builds on MyEastRen investment
  • Microsoft 365: Power BI for KPI dashboards; Teams for delivery collaboration; Power Automate for workflow automations
  • Digital Champions: Peer-to-peer change agents for staff adoption; one champion assigned per workstream
  • Scottish Digital Academy: Continued SAtSD and user research skills development in housing staff during the programme
  • IoT foundation: 50-property sensor pilot as proof of concept for proactive maintenance expansion
